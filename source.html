<h1 class="elementor-heading-title elementor-size-default">Hyperspectral Database – Early Access</h1> </div>
</div>
<div class="elementor-element elementor-element-ubqlkxo elementor-widget elementor-widget-text-editor" data-id="ubqlkxo" data-element_type="widget" data-widget_type="text-editor.default">
<div class="elementor-widget-container">
<div class="elementor-text-editor elementor-clearfix">
<p>The ICVL website is currently undergoing renovations, in order to allow rapid access to the database described in &#8220;<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20230128071909/http://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-319-46478-7_2">Sparse Recovery of Hyperspectral Signal from Natural RGB Images</a>&#8220;, we provide a list of flies below.</p><p>The database images were acquired using a Specim PS Kappa DX4 hyperspectral camera and a rotary stage for spatial scanning. At this time it contains 200 images and will continue to grow progressively.</p><p>Images were collected at 1392&#215;1300 spatial resolution over 519 spectral bands (400-1,000nm at roughly 1.25nm increments). The <strong>.raw</strong> files contain raw out-of-camera data in ENVI format and <strong>.hdr </strong>files contain the headers required to decode them. For your convenience, <strong>.mat</strong> files are provided, downsampled to 31 spectral channels from 400nm to 700nm at 10nm increments.</p><p>This page will be updated shortly with additional information.</p><p>For questions, comments and technical assistance, please comtact <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20230128071909/mailto:icvl@cs.bgu.ac.il">iCVL@cs.bgu.ac.il </a></p><p>When used, fully or partially, please cite &#8220;Arad and Ben-Shahar, Sparse Recovery of Hyperspectral Signal from Natural RGB Images, in the European Conference on Computer Vision, Amsterdam, The Netherlands, October 11–14, 2016&#8221;</p><pre>@inproceedings{arad_and_ben_shahar_2016_ECCV,
